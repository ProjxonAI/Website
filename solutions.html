Product vision

We’re building with focus and restraint

Projxon is still pre-launch, but we want to share the intent behind what’s coming: a local-first assistant that feels personal, respectful, and ready for everyday use.

What to expect

A glimpse at the first release

We're keeping the feature list intentionally tight. These are the pillars we’re polishing.

Personal companion

A conversational surface that remembers the right things, learns your tone, and feels approachable whether you're on desktop or phone.

Private by default

Local processing, optional sync, and transparent controls so you always know what stays on-device versus what travels.

Calm workspace

Clean UI, subtle motion, and a command palette feel. No noisy feeds, no distractions— just the context you need when you need it.

Sharable with intent

Lightweight exports and handoff-friendly threads so you can bring collaborators into the loop without revealing everything.

Roadmap

Our staged rollout plan

We're building in the open with a small waitlist. Here's how we're thinking about access.

Now — Private build

Internal tools, rapid prototyping, and design partner feedback. Expect weekly refreshes and occasional peeks on socials.

Next — Waitlist beta

A limited group of creators and teams with direct access to the people building Projxon. Rolling invites once we're confident in stability.

Later — Public launch

When the product feels trustworthy and delightful. You'll hear from us well before the launch if you're on the list.

Beyond — Platform

Thoughtful integrations and ways to extend Projxon without compromising the local-first promise.

Principles

Rules we refuse to break

These commitments shape every product decision while we're still pre-launch.

Privacy is product

We don’t bolt on security later. Data minimization and on-device compute come first, even if it slows us down.

Clarity over complexity

Every setting, screen, and interaction should explain itself. If it takes a doc to understand, we redesign it.

Design with people

We co-create with early adopters instead of throwing a polished surprise. Honest conversations lead to better tools.
